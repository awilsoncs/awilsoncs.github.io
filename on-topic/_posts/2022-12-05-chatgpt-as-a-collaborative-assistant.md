I spent the day working with ChatGPT, testing its limits, and exploring potential workflows that leverage its generative capability. This post will look at what worked, what didn't, show off an example of creative usage.

Instead of introducing [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com/chat) myself, I'll let it give its introduction:

> Could you introduce yourself to a technical audience that has never used you?

In moments, ChatGPT responded:

> Of course! My name is Assistant, and I am a large language model trained by OpenAI. I am designed to assist people with a wide variety of tasks, including answering questions, providing information, and generating text based on the input I receive. I am not a human, but I am designed to be able to communicate with people in a natural and intuitive way. Because I am a language model, I am particularly well-suited to understanding and generating text, but I can also provide information and answer questions within the scope of my training. I am always learning and improving, so please don't hesitate to ask me anything you'd like to know.

## What Worked Well

There are a few aspects of the service that work well.

### Holding a Conversation

ChatGPT is good at holding a conversation. I can ask it to iterate on a narrative or example, focus on different themes, and dive into specific topics. For instance, I used it to generate a job description for a technical lead. The output was more or less what I would expect to find on a job board:

> The Technical Lead is responsible for leading a team of software developers and ensuring that the team produces high-quality, reliable, and scalable code. This person will be responsible for overseeing the technical direction of the team, providing guidance and mentorship to team members, and making technical decisions that align with the business goals and objectives.

I clarified that we needed a tech lead skilled in designing new systems. It amended the previous listing to include some interesting lines:

> Design new systems and architectures that are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with the business needs

> Proven ability to design new systems and architectures that are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with the business needs

This interaction is just one example of how ChatGPT revises previous responses based on new input.

The responses are stateful, not just iterations. The system can leverage prior responses in later prompts:

> I'd like to write a technical test for this position.

ChatGPT gives a lengthy summary of an example technical test for a tech lead. It includes a coding challenge, a design exercise, a code review, and a behavioral segment. I ask it to provide a sample code review task. It generates a Java class that is overall well-structured but includes minor inefficiencies and problematic threading details. The integrity of this sort of technical output requires a close review by the user (as we saw when [StackOverflow temporarily banned responses generated by the service](https://meta.stackoverflow.com/questions/421831/temporary-policy-chatgpt-is-banned).)

### Asking Hypotheticals

ChatGPT seems wary of providing declarative answers to prompts:

> What does the first page of George Washington's diary say?

ChatGPT declines to answer, as it does not know George Washington's diary.

> What might the first page of George Washington's diary say?

ChatGPT still expresses that it cannot give a definitive answer but provides some speculation on the matter:

> The content of these diaries would likely include information about his military and political career, as well as his personal thoughts and observations. It is possible that the first page of his diary would include an introduction to the diary itself, or an entry about his daily activities or experiences.

Working in hypotheticals yields very positive results. However, after a certain point in the conversation, the system seems to understand that we're working in fiction, speaking in potential avenues rather than definitives. (see [World-building with ChatGPT](#world-building-with-chatgpt)).

### Selecting Options

I enjoyed the system's ability to present ideas. I could perform all the same interactions with the ideas, but importantly, I could ask the system to keep or integrate a specific idea into the dialogue. We could explore a path by recursively selecting options in increasing detail. See [What Isn't Great](#what-isnt-great) for a few improvements to this workflow that I would love to see.

### Specific Tasks

ChatGPT was good at performing a few specific tasks that would be useful to the average user. I would suggest ChatGPT as a tool to write the following:

* copy for websites and products
* proposals
* job descriptions
* user stories
* announcements
* other business writing

These types of writing are clearly well-represented in the training corpus, as the output is at the level I would expect from a human.

## What Isn't Great

### Restatement

ChatGPT is a wordy system. Responses to prompts can be multiple paragraphs long. After diving into the specifics of a topic, ChatGPT tends to restate prior information. This type of response is mostly clutter, as they usually occur when I'm trying to zoom out from a particular topic. Overall, it's not a big hurdle- sending it in a different direction takes another prompt. Ideally, ChatGPT would function like a colleague in a meeting, taking and sharing notes on decisions made so that they can be reviewed in real-time rather than waiting or asking for a restatement.

### Thread Continuity

Tangentially, I would like to drop and resume threads. If ChatGPT is to be my colleague, we need to be able to switch contexts when priorities change. Currently, all state is lost when the user resets the thread. Hosting all topics within a single thread is possible, but I've found two issues in doing so. The first is that ChatGPT seems to have a limit on the size of the prior state. After a long session, the system begins to take longer and longer to produce responses before eventually stopping entirely. OpenAI is getting flooded due to interest in this tool, so this problem may alleviate with time. The second issue is that the model experiences some bleed over. In other words, there needs to be a way to ask ChatGPT to discuss an unrelated but similar topic. It will continue to bring symbols from prior conversations into the new discussion. Both of these can be cured by resetting the thread, but as far as I understand, it's impossible to "reprime" ChatGPT with prior information so that we can continue an earlier line.

> Can we talk about a thread from earlier today?

> I'm sorry, but I am a large language model trained by OpenAI and do not have the ability to browse the internet or discuss specific threads from today or any other day. I can provide general information and answer questions to the best of my ability based on my training, but I do not have access to current events or specific discussions that may have taken place online. Is there something else I can help you with?

Both of these features would transform ChatGPT into a valuable daily coworker.

## World-building with ChatGPT

I wanted to show off some example usage. I spent about an hour diving into a new creative setting with ChatGPT earlier today. We explored the world's structure, themes, culture, threats, factions, symbols, and potential art direction. I was genuinely impressed by its ability to synthesize meaning throughout the conversation, freely combining ideas and connecting new topics to old ones. The experiment was lengthy and not suitable for even a long-form post. I've reproduced the dialogue to demonstrate a world-building flow using the tool.

> Hey, can we do some world-building together? This is for a physical guidebook to a new campaign setting.

> Sure! I'd love to help with some world-building. What would you like to start with? Do you have any specific ideas or themes in mind for this campaign setting?

> Yeah, I want the setting to be about ghost dinosaurs. Imagine Haunted Mansion meets Jurassic Park.

ChatGPT praises the concept and offers five ideas ranging from encounter locations, other ghostly creatures, abilities that ghost dinosaurs may have, and even a possible origin story for them:

> The ghost dinosaurs could be the result of some kind of ancient magical experiment gone wrong. Perhaps a powerful wizard or sorcerer, in their pursuit of knowledge and power, accidentally unleashed a curse that turned the dinosaurs into ghostly creatures. This curse could have been dormant for many centuries, only recently reawakened by some unforeseen event.

But the dinosaurs themselves aren't what I want to focus on.

> That all sounds great, but I really want to focus on the interpersonal drama surrounding the main characters in this world.

ChatGPT again has some ideas:

> The main characters could be part of a group of adventurers or explorers who are tasked with investigating the reappearance of the ghost dinosaurs and finding a way to stop them. However, their mission could be complicated by rivalries and personal conflicts within the group, as well as outside threats from other groups who have their own agendas.

That sounds reasonable. I tell ChatGPT that I like this idea best, and it expands on it. I like this one:

> The group's mission could be complicated by the actions of other characters who are not directly part of their team. For example, they could encounter innocent bystanders who are caught up in the chaos caused by the ghost dinosaurs, and who need the group's help to survive. They could also encounter powerful NPCs who could either help or hinder their progress, depending on how the characters choose to interact with them.

> I think one of the powerful NPCs is a wealthy benefactor.

> That's a great idea! A wealthy benefactor could provide the group with important resources and support, but could also have their own agendas and expectations that the group would have to navigate. Here are a few more ideas for how a wealthy benefactor could be incorporated into the story:

Frankly, I love how supportive it is. One of the ideas it provides is that the benefactor is an expert on ghost dinosaurs, a complex character that sometimes withholds information for an agenda unknown to the party. We meander down this path for a few messages before I ask it to address a related issue:

> I'd like to write an art description for the wealthy benefactor.

> Sure, I'd be happy to help with that! Here's a possible art description for the wealthy benefactor:

> The benefactor is an imposing figure, standing tall and commanding with an air of authority and intelligence. They are impeccably dressed in fine clothes and jewels, with a well-groomed beard and piercing eyes that seem to see everything. Despite their wealth and power, there is a hint of sadness and weariness in their face, as if they have seen and suffered much in their long life. They carry themselves with a confidence and grace that belies their age, and their words are carefully chosen and weighted with meaning.

> In terms of visual appearance, the benefactor could be depicted as a tall, middle-aged man with dark hair and a well-trimmed beard. He could be wearing a suit or other formal attire, with fine fabrics and details that reflect his wealth and status. He could also be carrying a walking stick or other symbol of his power and influence. In contrast to his elegant clothes, the benefactor's face could show signs of age and experience, with deep wrinkles and a hint of sadness in his eyes. Overall, the benefactor should be depicted as a complex and mysterious character, whose true intentions and motivations are not immediately clear.

I hope the tool's utility is plain to see in this exchange.
